Determine CTF parameters

Once the particles are boxed we may use ctfit to determine the CTF parameters. Knowing the CTF parameters is necessary in order to classify the particles and to align the particles within these classes. The CTF parameters include microscope parameters (voltage, spherical aberration coefficient, the defocus, B-factor and amplitude contrast) as well as the parameters that describe the noise present in the images (4 parameters using an empirical model). This information is written into the headers for the particle files and are read automatically by the classification and alignment programs.

The phase-flipping portion of the CTF correction process is also performed here. While this could be postponed until the amplitudes are corrected later in the reconstruction process, correcting the phases at this point means the particles can be used in reconstructions without CTF amplitude correction, and produce more accurate (but not perfect) results.
